# Framework Mapping

This tool is designed as a practical operational layer that aligns with several recognised AI security and governance frameworks.

## NIST AI Risk Management Framework

Relevant ideas used:

- Govern: define accountability, policy, roles, and oversight
- Map: inventory AI systems, data flows, use cases, and business context
- Measure: assess risk, control effectiveness, and AI security maturity
- Manage: implement controls, track remediation, and monitor residual risk

Reference: https://www.nist.gov/itl/ai-risk-management-framework

## NIST Generative AI Profile

Relevant ideas used:

- Generative AI risk identification
- Data protection and privacy considerations
- Synthetic content and hallucination risk
- Governance of GenAI use cases and controls
- Monitoring and continuous improvement

Reference: https://www.nist.gov/itl/ai-risk-management-framework

## ISO/IEC 42001

Relevant ideas used:

- AI management system approach
- Accountability and governance structure
- AI policy, roles, risk review, and continuous improvement
- Operational control of AI systems

Reference: https://www.iso.org/standard/81230.html

## ISO/IEC 23894

Relevant ideas used:

- AI risk identification
- Risk treatment
- Risk monitoring
- Documentation and accountability

Reference: https://www.iso.org/standard/77304.html

## OWASP Top 10 for LLM Applications

Relevant ideas used:

- Prompt injection
- Sensitive information disclosure
- Insecure output handling
- Supply chain risks
- Excessive agency
- Vector and embedding weaknesses
- Insecure plugin/tool design

Reference: https://owasp.org/www-project-top-10-for-large-language-model-applications/

## MITRE ATLAS

Relevant ideas used:

- Adversarial threats against AI-enabled systems
- Attack techniques and detection opportunities
- Threat-informed defence for AI systems

Reference: https://atlas.mitre.org/

## CSA AI Controls Matrix

- Cloud and AI control domains
- Data governance
- Security monitoring

- Third-party risk

## EU AI Act Concepts

Relevant ideas used:

- Risk-based AI governance
- High-risk AI system thinking
- Documentation, transparency, and oversight
- Human oversight and accountability

Reference: https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/
